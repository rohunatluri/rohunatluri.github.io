Work/Internships

Naval Postgraduate School

Paid Research Intern • June 2015 - August 2015

Improving and innovating upon the progress of my previous summer at NPS, I worked with astronauts, professors, military officers, and PhD students to design, program, and launch satellite hardware into a near-space environment using a high altitude balloon (100,000+ ft). Working with a team of mentors and interns, I programmed various sensors, cameras, and radios using a Raspberry Pi 2 Model B. Our team was the first from NPS to implement a Raspberry Pi in a high altitude balloon, constantly maintaining two-way communication with multiple groundstations and transmitting telemetric data, including GPS position, Pi memory level, current battery capacities, internal and external temperature, humidity, and accelerometric orientation. In addition, we programmed the Pi to respond to a groundstation command to separate the payload from the balloon by driving a linear actuator.

Naval Postgraduate School

Paid Research Intern • June 2014 - August 2014

I worked with a team of interns under Dr. Jim Newman to design, program, and launch a high altitude balloon. Using a Beaglebone Black microcomputer, we integrated various sensors into the payload along with an APRS radio. I took on the role of the head of the payload design team, organizing and leading three other interns in the development of the payload structure, parachute deployment, and balloon release mechanism utilizing various tools including SolidWorks and a 3D printer. In addition, I worked with other interns to program sensors and dice cameras using Python.
